Bring the Work

Atlanta-area stories of resilience, brought to life through performance.

A live multidisciplinary work blending chamber orchestra, contemporary dance, videography, and real community voices.

About the Project

Art as civic memory.

Rooted in Newnan, Georgia's recovery after the 2021 EF-4 tornado, Seasons of Strength uses live music, movement, projected imagery, and recorded oral histories to transform local stories into an experience of resilience, remembrance, and connection.

Created and directed by Gwendolyn Kuhlmann, the work reflects a culture of collaboration, care, and collective action that defines the Atlanta region. It functions as art as civic memory — preserving lived experience while inviting audiences into a shared human story.

The Experience

Four elements, one story.

01
Live Chamber Orchestra
Musicians of the LaGrange Symphony Orchestra, performing live alongside the story.
02
Contemporary Dance
Southern Arc Dance, translating testimony into movement.
03
Videography
Documentary footage of the disaster and the community's response, projected live.
04
Real Recorded Voices
Over 100 community members, telling the story in their own words.

The result is immersive and emotionally immediate — accessible across cultures and languages.

Programming Details

Scalable for any stage.

Full Production

Presentation Options

Collaborators

Created & directed by Gwendolyn Kuhlmann.

In collaboration with choreographer Paulo Manso de Sousa, videographer Jake Barton, musicians of the LaGrange Symphony Orchestra, Southern Arc Dance (professional, community, and high school dancers), and the voices of over 100 community members.

Why This Work Matters

Real stories, translated into shared artistic language.

Seasons of Strength preserves real stories from a disaster-impacted community and translates them into a form audiences of any background can meet. For presenters, that means:

Visual & Emotional Impact
A strong, immediate experience for a live audience.
Cultural Grounding
Clear roots in the Atlanta region, with a story that travels.
Universal Themes
Resilience, community, and recovery read across audiences.
Flexible Programming
Scalable from a 20-minute excerpt to the full 90-minute production.
