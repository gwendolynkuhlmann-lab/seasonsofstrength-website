Our Story

Who we are, and how we made it.

The founding story, the process, the people, and the community that made the first show possible.

Who We Are

It started with a drive through Newnan.

In April 2021, weeks after an EF-4 tornado tore through Coweta County, Gwendolyn Kuhlmann drove through the parts of Newnan the storm had hit hardest. She wasn't there to document damage. She was there because an idea had already taken hold: that something beautiful could be made out of this — not despite what the community had been through, but out of it.

That drive became Seasons of Strength: a multidisciplinary performance organization based in Newnan, Georgia, building oral histories from disaster-affected communities into live performances that integrate orchestral music, contemporary dance, and documentary video.

Gwendolyn is the creator, writer, director, and producer of Seasons of Strength. She's a classically trained opera singer, creative director, and brand storyteller, with an MA from the University of the Arts Berlin.

How We Made It

One hundred voices, gathered over years.

The oral history process started small — recordings made with basic software at the Newnan-Coweta History Center. With support from Caring Communities Foundation, it grew into a WABE listening booth and traveling recording sessions that met people where they were. A handful of formal documentary interview sessions anchored the work; the real time investment was in what came after — the months Jake Barton spent in post-production, shaping over one hundred recorded voices into a single continuous story.

Some future collaborations with Newnan's youth are still ahead of us. We're building toward deeper youth engagement as the project grows — not as something already delivered, but as where we're headed next.
